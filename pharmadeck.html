Mapping the Friction: From Intake to Drop-off in Regional Holdings

Phase 1: The Intake

The origin of the prescription data flow.

Initial Processing

Step A: E-Prescription

The electronic prescription arrives securely from the medical provider, initiating the data cycle within the pharmacy system.

Step B: Patient Data

Technicians perform data entry and the initial profile match, preparing the script for complex insurance routing.

The Danger Zone

Phase 2: "Frankenstein" Adjudication

Step C: Real-Time Eligibility (RTE) Check

The system attempts an automated connection to major Pharmacy Benefit Managers (PBMs) like Caremark or Express Scripts.

This is where standard logic hits complex regulatory and payer walls, creating immense friction.

⚠️ The Bottleneck

11.81%

Claims hit a hard stop.

This is Step D. The revenue flow breaks completely. The legacy Pharmacy Management System (PMS) is structurally incapable of parsing complex denial codes automatically.

Why The Flow Breaks

Step Therapy & PA Flags

Prior Authorization (PA) hurdles and strict step therapy requirements stall the script, blocking automated dispensing and demanding clinical override.

Out-of-Network Errors

Data mismatches, rigid network barriers, or missing proprietary logic trigger automatic rejections that standard PMS tools cannot resolve.

Phase 3: Manual Rescue

  • Step E: The Pharmacist or Tech is forced to step away from the counter and the patient.
  • Step F: They must log into 3 to 5 separate, unintegrated web portals.
  • They manually apply Secondary Insurance, Manufacturer Rebates, and PBM overrides.

The Time Drain

14 Min

Wasted per transaction.

Highly paid clinical staff are forced into basic data-entry triage. Across hundreds of daily scripts, 14 minutes per complex claim scales into massive, unrecoverable labor losses.

The Cash Drain

$25-$57

Lost in administrative rework.

This is pure margin erased directly from the holding's bottom line. Manual intervention entirely destroys the profitability of the prescription.

Phase 4: The Drop-Off

Step G: The Co-pay logic is finally calculated.

Medication Abandonment

  • 60% Abandonment
  • 40% Retained / Filled

If the manual adjudication process took too long at the counter, or if the resulting co-pay calculation remains over $500, patients will simply walk away from the script.
